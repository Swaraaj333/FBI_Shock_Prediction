Key Achievements of the Model

1. Translational Emphasis: Identified future shock events with an overall accuracy of ~78% and a high stable-state recall of 89%. The prediction horizon (up to 8 hours, as proven in complex test cases) is designed to provide actionable lead time for clinicians.

2. Broad clinical applicability by design. The model is designed to leverage the broad utility of the Shock Index, calculating it dynamically to serve as an early indicator of cardiovascular deterioration.

3. Monitor-only early prediction of Hemodynamic Shock (1-8 hours lead time) from routinely available, non-invasive ICU vital signs (Heart Rate, Systolic BP, MAP, Resp Rate, SpO2).

4. Real-world development and evaluation, using the MIMIC-IV clinical database (representing highly diverse ICU patient stays) for robust model training, feature engineering, and validation.

5. Robustness to Clinical Sparsity and sensor disconnections, demonstrating the ability to maintain predictive accuracy during periods of missing data:
   a. Utilized Last-Observation-Carried-Forward (LOCF) for temporal imputation.
   b. Demonstrated success on 'sudden crash' and 'missing data' external test cohorts.

6. Eradication of Target Leakage via temporal target shifting, ensuring the algorithm forecasts future deterioration rather than merely diagnosing current, obvious distress.

7. Mechanistically interpretable signatures of deterioration, supported by correlation analyses identifying physiologically accurate markers (e.g., strong negative correlation with declining Mean Arterial Pressure).

8. Release of an end-to-end Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS), integrating a robust Flask Inference API with a fully interactive Streamlit frontend for batch processing and real-time bedside triage.
